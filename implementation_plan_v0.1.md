# Implementation Plan v0.2 — Segment-Dependent Chunk Length via a Video Stability Predictor

Companion to `adaptive_chunking_hypothesis_v0.4.tex`. Principle: **train only the stability
predictor head; reuse everything else.** The video model has exactly one job: given recent
frames, predict whether the current segment is open-loop stable or unstable. It is trained
supervised on labels generated by the simulator. Executed chunk length `k` is an
inference-time knob, so the policy is never retrained.

---

## Pipeline in one line

sim oracle → per-timestep stability labels → supervised training of (frozen video encoder +
small head) → at runtime the predictor watches recent frames → binary k switch.

## 0. What is reused vs. built

| Component | Source | Reused / Built |
|---|---|---|
| Chunking policy | Diffusion Policy (real-stanford repo, robomimic configs); ACT for stage 2 | **Reused** (retrain from released config if needed; hours on 1 GPU) |
| Video encoder | V-JEPA 2 ViT-L, frozen (`facebook/vjepa2-*`); single-frame DINOv2 as ablation | **Reused, frozen** |
| Stability head | Attentive pooling + 2-layer MLP on encoder features (+ proprio MLP) — V-JEPA's standard attentive-probe protocol | **Built (tiny; the only trained component)** |
| Label generator | MuJoCo `get_state/set_state` perturb-and-replay script | **Built (a script, not a model)** |
| Benchmark + demos | robomimic (Square-ph, Tool-Hang-ph, Can-ph); ALOHA sim insertion; Transport/FurnitureSim later | **Reused** |
| Real-world label source (later) | Rollout-divergence of an action-conditioned world model (V-JEPA 2-AC / DINO-WM) replacing the sim oracle | **Deferred** |

## 1. Label generation (simulator oracle — ground truth, no learning)

**Standard-tool footing:** the ground-truth quantity is the **finite-time Lyapunov exponent
(FTLE)** — the textbook estimator of local exponential divergence (Benettin et al. 1980;
used for RL policy robustness in arXiv:2410.10674). Our perturb-and-replay with fixed actions
is the *open-loop FTLE*; with the policy reacting it is the *closed-loop FTLE*. The
Simchowitz-line papers (2503.09722, 2507.09061) provide no empirical classification
procedure — they assume EISS (their Assumption 3.1) and build synthetic systems stable by
construction — so the per-segment labeling protocol is ours, but every ingredient is
standard and citable (FTLE; contraction analysis, Lohmiller & Slotine 1998; the IL
community's open-loop-replay success test). Setting σ_u = policy validation RMSE makes the
label exactly the paper's error-compounding gain: does an injected error of the size the
policy actually makes get amplified (J_TRAJ ≫ J_DEMO) or absorbed over the segment horizon.

For each demo timestep t (subsample every 2–5 steps), in MuJoCo:

**Open-loop label** — property of the plant (robot + low-level controller + environment):
1. Reset sim to the demo state at t.
2. Nominal branch: replay the demo's recorded actions a_{t:t+K}. **Final recipe K = 24**
   (the v0.1 spec said 16; the probe study moved it — see "Choosing K" below).
3. Perturbed branches (N = 8): identical, except Gaussian noise on the **first action only**,
   σ_u = the trained policy's validation action RMSE (probe with the error the policy will
   actually inject).
4. d(τ) = state-space distance (ee pose + object pose) between branches; fit
   λ_ol(t) = OLS slope of log d(τ) vs τ.
5. y_ol(t) = **unstable** if λ_ol > +δ, **stable** if λ_ol < −δ, deadband inherits neighbor;
   median-filter over ~3 stamps → contiguous segments. Log contact flags as a sanity channel.

**Closed-loop label** (secondary/diagnostic): same perturbed pairs, but in the perturbed branch
the trained policy acts from its own observations with replanning every step → λ_cl(t),
y_cl(t). The λ_cl vs λ_ol comparison per segment verifies the Section-1 mechanism (feedback
injects estimation error in contracting segments; ordering flips in expansive ones).

**Choosing K (the probe window: how many steps we watch the error for).** K is bracketed
by two lower bounds and two upper bounds, then fixed empirically inside that bracket:
- *Lower bound 1 — fit quality past the controller transient.* λ is an OLS slope of
  log d(τ); the first ~0.2–0.5 s (4–10 steps at 20 Hz) is dominated by the OSC tracking
  controller's settling transient, so K must leave enough points beyond it or the slope
  measures the transient, not the regime.
- *Lower bound 2 — decision relevance.* The label certifies "safe to commit a chunk
  open-loop," so the window must cover at least the longest chunk the controller may
  execute: K ≥ k_long = T_p = 16 for DP.
- *Upper bound 1 — locality.* K must stay well below typical segment dwell time,
  otherwise the window straddles a regime boundary and the label blends two regimes
  (median filtering cannot repair a mixed window).
- *Upper bound 2 — staying in the exponential regime.* The fit assumes d(τ) ≈ σ_u·e^{λτ}.
  Over long windows divergence saturates (workspace scale, new contact events) and
  log d flattens, biasing λ toward 0; larger σ_u tightens this bound, coupling K and σ_u.
- *Empirical pick inside the bracket:* the 6-variant probe study (30 square demos)
  selected K = 24 (1.2 s) with position-only perturbation: fixture-contact stamps above
  deadband 36.6% vs 12.7% at K = 16, free-space false positives 1.2%. Stage-0 synthetic
  check: the labeler at these settings recovers the true λ to 4 decimals. K is a
  per-embodiment constant (set by controller settling time + chunk horizon), re-derived
  for ALOHA in stage 2; N/K/σ_u/δ sensitivity ablation is scheduled in stage 2.

**Coverage:** generate labels along BOTH demo states and trained-policy rollout states
(including failed rollouts) — for rollouts, the "recorded actions" replayed in the nominal
branch are the policy's executed actions. This ensures the predictor is trained on the state
distribution it will actually face at runtime, not just the demo tube.

Cost: pure sim rollouts, embarrassingly parallel, no gradients. ~200 demos (+ a similar number
of policy rollouts) × ~1 label/2–5 steps × (N+1) × K short rollouts — hours on CPU workers.

## 2. The stability predictor (the trained component)

- Input: last 16 frames (the video clip) + proprioception.
- Backbone: **frozen V-JEPA 2** encoder. Rationale: stability is a *dynamics* property —
  contact, incipient slip, constrained motion are motion cues, visible in a clip and not in a
  single frame; V-JEPA 2's pretraining objective (masked latent *video* prediction) is exactly
  motion-predictive features. Single-frame DINOv2 head is the ablation that proves the
  temporal claim.
- Head: attentive pooling + 2-layer MLP (V-JEPA attentive-probe recipe, reused code), proprio
  concatenated after pooling.
- Outputs: p(OL-unstable); auxiliary λ̂_ol regression; optional p(CL-unstable), λ̂_cl.
- Optional ablation: LoRA on the last encoder blocks if the frozen probe underfits.

## 3. Loss functions

L = BCE_w(p_ol, y_ol) + α·Huber(λ̂_ol, λ_ol) [+ β·(same pair for CL)]

- **BCE (class-weighted)** on the open-loop regime is the decision loss — contact/unstable
  timesteps are the minority class, so weight or balance-sample.
- **Huber on λ** is auxiliary: calibrates the head, and gives the continuous-k rule for free
  later (k ≈ c/|λ̂|) as an ablation. α = 0.5 to start.
- CL outputs are trained from the start (they enable the full three-case runtime rule and the
  mechanism analysis); β = 0.5 alongside the OL terms.
- Policy losses untouched (DP: DDPM MSE; ACT: L1 + β·KL). Encoder frozen.

## 4. Runtime controller

At each replan: encoder + head on the last 16 frames → regime → switch:
```
OL-stable                 →  k = k_long  (policy's standard chunk: DP Ta = 8–16, ACT = 100)
OL-unstable & CL-stable   →  k = 1      (Markovian, per-step feedback)
neither                   →  k = 1 + flag (no execution-side fix exists here; the remedy is
                                           data-side noise injection per Theorem 2 — Stage 2)
```
MVP may collapse to the first two rows (binary switch on the OL head) since the third case is
rare in these benchmarks, but both heads are trained from the start so the full rule is free.
Hysteresis: two consecutive agreeing predictions to switch. No derived k*: the empirical k*
per segment comes from the Stage-1 sweep (smallest k where success plateaus in stable
segments). Continuous rules are ablations.

## 5. Datasets

- **No segmentation needed — segmentation is an OUTPUT, not an input.** All these datasets
  ship as continuous, unannotated demo trajectories. We never segment them by hand or by
  semantic phase: the per-timestep FTLE labels, after thresholding + median filtering, ARE
  the segments (stability regime is the only segment notion the hypothesis uses). Run the
  labeler over everything and let the regimes emerge. Sim contact flags serve only as an
  independent sanity check, and alignment between stability segments and semantic phases
  (reach/grasp/insert) is a qualitative result, not a dependency.
- **Two different data roles:** (a) *predictor training pool* — use ALL tasks, short- and
  long-horizon alike: the label is local (recent frames → local regime), so episode length
  is irrelevant to label semantics, and short tasks add cheap regime diversity while
  breaking the "late-in-episode ⇒ contact" shortcut. (b) *Hypothesis-test tasks* — only
  long-horizon tasks are informative, because the claim is that no constant k works when a
  task contains BOTH regimes; single-regime short tasks can't dissociate, but they make
  clean controls (mostly-stable task → long chunks should win/tie; contact-heavy task →
  k = 1 should win/tie).
- **Predictor training pool (Panda/OSC, one shared predictor):**
  robomimic Lift-ph, Can-ph, Square-ph, Tool-Hang-ph (all 200-demo PH sets);
  MimicGen D0–D1 generated sets across its task suite, including short tasks
  (coffee, threading, stack) for contact-event diversity; FurnitureSim one_leg
  demonstrations. Rule: the pool feeds the predictor ONLY; held-out tasks below
  never enter training.
- **Label-quality evaluation (calibration + generalization, not policy rollout):**
  calibration vs sim ground-truth FTLE on Square-ph and Tool-Hang-ph; Can-ph as the
  mostly-stable control; task-transfer test = head trained on Square only → labels
  scored on Tool-Hang. Embodiment-transfer tier (stage 2): ALOHA sim insertion +
  transfer-cube, separate action head, labels recomputed from scratch (temporal
  ensembling OFF — it reinstates per-step feedback; on/off is an ablation).
- **Policy rollout, short horizon:** Square-ph, Tool-Hang-ph (primary; reference DP
  numbers); Can-ph as negative control — the schedule should show ~zero gap.
- **Policy rollout, long horizon:** MimicGen three_piece_assembly, nut_assembly,
  kitchen, coffee_preparation (the alternation-count axis; headline scaling curve);
  FurnitureSim one_leg (contact-heavy showcase); LIBERO-Long paired with LIBERO-Pro
  perturbation levels (reviewer-legible benchmark + headroom).
- **Excluded (stated deliberately):** robomimic Transport (bimanual, demoted to
  stage 2); CALVIN (TTIC audit consistency findings).
- **Priority order:** same-embodiment (Panda/OSC) first — robomimic ph (running) →
  MimicGen → FurnitureSim; LIBERO and ALOHA after.
- **Real robot (later):** no sim oracle → labels from world-model rollout divergence
  (V-JEPA 2-AC: paired rollouts, same actions, perturbed first action, slope of log latent
  divergence) or from hardware perturb-and-replay on a subsample. Predictor architecture
  unchanged.

## 6. Experiment ladder (one money plot per stage)

- **Stage 0 (days, no GPU):** synthetic piecewise-linear system from the tex Caveat section.
  Optimal-k profile tracks the contraction profile; success degrades as k→1 in stable
  segments.
- **Stage 1a — key plot:** forced-k-per-segment sweep on Square/Tool-Hang using the *oracle
  labels directly* (predictor not yet in the loop; isolates the hypothesis from predictor
  quality). Heatmap of success over (k_stable, k_unstable); prediction: best corner is
  (large, small) and the k_stable = 1 column is *bad* — the sign prediction nobody else makes.
- **Stage 1b:** predictor quality — AUROC / calibration of p(OL-unstable) vs held-out oracle
  labels; alignment with contact flags; cross-task generalization; video-vs-single-frame
  ablation.
- **Stage 1c:** full closed loop — predictor-driven k switch vs every fixed k and vs
  policy-side baselines. Plus the dissociation cases:
  (a) contact with clean unimodal visuals → policy confident, environment unstable →
  entropy/variance baselines over-commit, ours shortens;
  (b) free space with induced bimodal demos → policy uncertain, environment stable →
  baselines shorten (and per Section 2, fail), ours commits.
- **Stage 2:** ACT/ALOHA replication; long-horizon task; ablations (continuous k rule,
  temporal ensembling, noise injection into unstable-segment demos per Theorem 2, label
  hyperparams N/K/σ_u/δ).

## 7. Baselines (all inference-time, same trained DP policy)

- Fixed k ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8, 16}.
- AAC-style: execute low-entropy prefix (DP proxy: variance across resampled chunks).
- DVAC-style: truncate on rising denoising variance.
- BID-style per-step resampling.

## 8. Risks and mitigations

- **Label noise near boundaries** → deadband δ, median filter, subsample stride.
- **Perturbation scale**: too small → noise floor; too large → leaves linear regime.
  σ_u = policy validation RMSE + a sensitivity ablation.
- **Class imbalance** (unstable is minority) → weighted BCE / balanced sampling.
- **Predictor learns task-position shortcut** ("late in episode ⇒ contact") instead of
  dynamics → cross-task generalization test (§5); shuffle-clip control.
- **ACT temporal ensembling confound** → off by default, ablated.
- **Sim-label ↔ real gap** (later) → world-model-divergence labels replace the oracle; same
  head, same losses.

## 9. Compute

Single A100 or less: DP ≤ ~12 h/task (or released checkpoints); label generation is CPU-hours;
head training is minutes–hours (frozen backbone, cached features). Stage 0 is a laptop.
Stages 0–1 ≈ 4–6 weeks wall-clock.
